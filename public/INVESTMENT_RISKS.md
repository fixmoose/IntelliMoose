## General Investment Risks

### 1. Total Loss of Investment
- Early-stage companies have a high failure rate (70-90% fail within 10 years)
- There is a significant risk that you could lose 100% of your investment
- IntelliMoose is pre-revenue and pre-product, increasing risk

### 2. Illiquidity
- No established market for resale of securities
- Expect to hold investment for 7-10+ years minimum
- Cannot easily convert investment to cash
- No guarantee of liquidity event (acquisition or IPO)

### 3. Dilution
- Future funding rounds may dilute your ownership percentage
- Down rounds could reduce your investment value
- Company may issue additional securities without your consent

### 4. Limited Operating History
- Company has no operating history or track record
- Management team lacks proven success in this specific market
- No established customer base or revenue

## Company-Specific Risks

### Technology Risks

**1. Product Development Failure**
- Product may not function as intended
- Technical challenges may prove insurmountable
- Hardware integration may be more complex than anticipated
- AI models may not perform adequately on edge devices

**2. Manufacturing Challenges**
- Reliance on Raspberry Pi 5 and third-party components
- Supply chain disruptions (as experienced in 2020-2022)
- Manufacturing defects or quality control issues
- Inability to scale production cost-effectively

**3. Technology Obsolescence**
- Rapid pace of AI advancement may render our approach outdated
- Competitors may develop superior technology
- Open-source models may evolve faster than we can adapt

### Market Risks

**1. Market Adoption**
- Target customers may not adopt AI-powered smart glasses
- Privacy concerns may limit market acceptance
- Training requirements may create adoption barriers
- ROI may be insufficient to justify purchase

**2. Market Size**
- Total Addressable Market (TAM) estimates may be inflated
- Actual serviceable market may be smaller than projected
- Market growth may be slower than anticipated

**3. Competitive Landscape**
- Major tech companies (Google, Meta, Apple) may enter market
- Well-funded startups may emerge with competitive products
- Established players may copy our approach
- Price competition may erode margins

### Financial Risks

**1. Capital Requirements**
- Company may require additional funding beyond current raise
- Future funding may not be available or may be on unfavorable terms
- Burn rate may exceed projections
- Revenue ramp may be slower than anticipated

**2. Revenue Model**
- Hardware-first revenue model has low margins
- Recurring revenue streams unproven
- Enterprise sales cycles are long (6-18 months)
- Customer acquisition costs may be higher than projected

**3. Path to Profitability**
- Company is pre-revenue with no clear path to profitability
- Break-even may take 3-5+ years
- Operating expenses may exceed revenue for extended period

### Regulatory & Legal Risks

**1. Intellectual Property**
- Patent applications pending (not yet granted)
- Patents may not be granted or may be challenged
- Freedom to operate not fully assessed
- Potential IP infringement claims from competitors

**2. Privacy Regulations**
- GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy laws may restrict functionality
- Future regulations may impact business model
- Data handling requirements may increase costs
- Liability for data breaches or misuse

**3. Safety Certifications**
- ANSI Z87.1, FCC, CE certifications required but not yet obtained
- Certification process may be costly and time-consuming
- Failure to obtain certifications would prevent market entry
- Ongoing compliance costs

**4. Liability**
- Product liability for workplace injuries or accidents
- Professional liability if AI provides incorrect information
- Cybersecurity liability for data breaches
- Insurance costs may be prohibitive

### Management & Operational Risks

**1. Key Person Dependence**
- Heavy reliance on founder/CEO Dejan Obradovic
- Loss of key personnel could be detrimental
- Difficulty attracting and retaining top talent
- Limited management team experience

**2. Execution Risk**
- Company may fail to execute on business plan
- Product development may take longer than expected
- Go-to-market strategy may prove ineffective
- Operational challenges may arise

**3. Corporate Governance**
- Founder control may limit investor influence
- Limited board oversight and governance
- Potential conflicts of interest
- Lack of independent directors

## Exit-Related Risks

### 1. No Guarantee of Exit
- No assurance of acquisition or IPO
- Exit timeline highly uncertain (typically 7-10+ years)
- Exit valuation may be lower than investment
- Company may remain private indefinitely

### 2. Acquisition Risks
- Potential acquirers may not be interested
- Acquisition price may not provide positive return
- Acquisition may be blocked by regulatory concerns
- Earn-outs or contingent payments may not materialize

### 3. IPO Risks
- Company may never be large enough for IPO
- IPO market conditions may be unfavorable
- Lock-up periods may delay liquidity
- Public market valuation may disappoint
