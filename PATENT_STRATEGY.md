### Non-Obviousness Argument

Why a skilled practitioner wouldn't have thought of this:

1. **LLMs too large for edge** - Llama 3.2 only became viable for edge inference in 2024
2. **RAG typically cloud-based** - Vector search assumed to require server infrastructure
3. **Trade manuals not digitized** - Converting PDFs to embeddings is non-trivial
4. **Multi-device pairing complex** - Abstracting across smart glasses brands requires novel protocol
5. **Offline operation uncommon** - Most AI products assume internet connectivity

**Conclusion:** An ordinary skilled person wouldn't combine these elements in this specific configuration. Patent-worthy.

## Patent Prosecution Strategy

### Claims Hierarchy

**Broad Claims (harder to get, more valuable):**
- "A system for delivering offline AI expertise..."
- "A method for portable knowledge retrieval..."

**Narrow Claims (easier to get, still protective):**
- "A pocket AI device specifically configured for electrical trades..."
- "A RAG system optimized for NEC code retrieval..."

**Strategy:** File broad claims first, fall back to narrow claims during prosecution.

### Office Actions Response Plan

Expect USPTO to cite prior art like:
- Offline language models (refute: not portable, not trade-specific)
- Smart glasses (refute: cloud-dependent, not expertise-focused)
- Knowledge bases (refute: require internet, not pre-loaded)

**Prepared Arguments:**
- Combination of elements is novel
- Specific implementation details are non-obvious
- Demonstrated commercial viability proves utility

## FAQ

**Q: Can I file the patent myself (pro se)?**
A: Technically yes, but not recommended. Patent law is complex, and poorly drafted claims can be worthless. Budget $2K-5K for attorney help on provisional.

**Q: What if someone already has a patent on offline AI?**
A: Unlikely. Even if they do, your specific implementation (trade manuals + RAG + multi-device) is novel. Continuation patents or design-around claims still viable.

**Q: Should I wait until product is perfect before filing?**
A: NO. File provisional ASAP with current design. You can refine claims during the 12-month provisional period.

**Q: What if I can't afford $10K-15K for utility patent?**
A: File provisional now ($2K-5K). Raise seed round. Use $40K from $2.5M raise (1.6%) for utility patent. This is standard.

**Q: Can investors steal my idea if I share before patent?**
A: Provisional patent gives you priority date. Share with investors after filing provisional. Use NDA for extra caution, but most VCs won't sign NDAs.

**Q: What happens if USPTO rejects my patent?**
A: Common. Most patents go through 1-3 office actions (rejections). Attorney responds with narrowed claims or arguments. ~90% of patents eventually issue after revisions.
